The 2005 Greenpoint-Williamsburg Rezoning

In 2005, much of Greenpoint and Williamsburg was rezoned from manufacturing and light industrial use to residential and commercial. This shift responded to decades of industrial decline, an influx of artists and newcomers from Manhattan, and the Bloomberg administration’s vision for a revitalized waterfront. Zoning regulations played a key role in shaping what could be built—and have since helped transform North Brooklyn’s waterfront into what it is today.
